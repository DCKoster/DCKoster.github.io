Analysis Variations

For the analysis of both variations, the same strategy as was used in the analysis of the threesum will be used. Because there is an infinite amount of root worlds, one root world for both variations is chosen as example.

Analysis of the Four Person "Who has the Sum?" Riddle

For the Four Person "Who has the Sum?", the root world will be \( (3, 1, 1, 1) \). This root world is in the form \( (n_a, n_b, n_c, n_d) \) with \( max = n_a \). As explained in the variations, it is connected to \( t = (n_a, [n_a + n_c + n_d], n_c, n_d) \) through \(w \sim_b t\), \( u = (n_a, n_b, [n_a + n_b + n_d], n_d) \) through \(w \sim_c u\), and \( v = (n_a, n_b, n_c, [n_a + n_b + n_c]) \) through \(w \sim_d v\). The worlds will continue to branch out, with each world having one relation for agent a (Achmed), one for agent b (Bert), one for agent c (Carl), and one for agent d (Dana). The root world of this part of the Kripke Model can be seen in figure 1

The first public announcement of Achmed removes the world in which it does not hold, which is \( (3, 1, 1, 1) \). The resulting state can be seen in figure 2.

The second announcement by Bert and third announcement by Carl both also remove the worlds where they do not hold. These are \( (3, 5, 1, 1) \) for Bert's announcement, and \( (3, 1, 5, 1) \) and \( (3, 5, 9, 1) \) for Carl's announcement. The resulting state after the second and third announcements can be seen in figures 3 and 4 respectively.

The fourth announcement by Dana, the new agent in this variation, has a bigger impact on the Kripke Model than the previous announcements. The worlds which will be removed as the announcement does not hold are: \( (3, 1, 1, 5) \), \( (3, 1, 5, 9) \), \( (3, 5, 1, 9) \), \( (3, 5, 9, 17) \). The resulting state after the announcemnt can be seen in figure 5.

The fifth announcement is Achmed announcing he knows his number. The worlds where this announcement holds are highlighted in red in figure 6. These are: \( (7, 1, 1, 5) \), \( (7, 1, 5, 1) \), \( (7, 5, 1, 1) \), \( (15, 1, 5, 9) \), \( (15, 5, 9, 1) \), \( (15, 5, 1, 9) \), and \( (31, 5, 9, 17) \).
